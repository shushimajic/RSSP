üìã Your First 4 Weeks Checklist

Click on each task to mark it complete. Take your time‚Äîyou don't need to do everything at once!

Week 1: Register for Government Services

Why? These are the foundation for everything else‚Äîmoney, health, housing.

  • Visit Centrelink (inside a Services Australia office) to register for income support
  • Ask about your Customer Reference Number (CRN)‚Äîyou'll need this for almost everything
  • Find your nearest office at servicesaustralia.gov.au/locations

Week 1‚Äì2: Enrol in Medicare

Why? Free or cheap healthcare for you and your family.

  • Visit a Medicare office (usually inside Centrelink) 7‚Äì10 days after arrival
  • Bring your passport and visa documents
  • You'll get a Medicare card in about 2 weeks

Week 1‚Äì2: Open an Australian Bank Account

Why? You need this to get paid, pay bills, and manage money safely.

  • Go to a major bank (Commonwealth, Westpac, ANZ, NAB) with your passport and visa
  • Ask for a proof-of-address letter from your housing provider if needed
  • They'll help you open an account on the spot‚Äîit's free!

Week 1: Connect with a Settlement Provider

Why? They're expert helpers who will guide you through everything and can help with housing, language classes, and more.

  • AMES Australia ‚Äì accommodation, English classes, job training ames.net.au
  • Uniting Vic.Tas ‚Äì housing, food, community support unitingvictas.org.au
  • SkillPath ‚Äì tertiary education & refugee student settlement skillpath.org.au

Week 2‚Äì3: Get a Local Doctor

Why? For health checks, vaccinations, and sick care.

  • Look for a bulk-billing GP (free doctor visit) near you using Google Maps
  • Call and ask if they accept new patients and have interpreters available
  • Book an appointment‚Äîyour first visit is often a free health check

Week 2‚Äì3: Get a Myki Card

Why? Your ticket for all trams, trains, and buses in Melbourne.

  • Buy a card at any convenience store or supermarket (costs about $6)
  • Top it up with money (e.g., $20 for about a week of travel)
  • Tip: Trams are FREE in the CBD (city center)!

Week 3‚Äì4: Meet a Local Migrant Resource Centre

Why? They offer free advice, material help (food, clothing), and can connect you to local community.

See the "Key Support Services" section in the app for centres near you.
